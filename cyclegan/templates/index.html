CycleGANs

CycleGANs aims to solve image-to-image translation, a type of computer vision problem where the idea is to convert one image to another "style", while trying to maintain as much of the image context as possible. An example would be converting images of landscapes during summer to look like the winter and vice-a-versa:

The figure above was actually taken from the CycleGAN paper by Zhu et al.. Their project site offers more details and examples on their work. Definitely check it out, CycleGANs are a super cool technique and they've got some interesting applications, such as converting Fortnite gameplay to PUBG!

Putting it all together (and a demo!)

Another area where CycleGANs have shown remarkable results are converting real-world photos into different art styles. One of the artists that this has worked for is (you guess it!) Vincent van Gogh. This demo uses the trained model from the original paper.

You can either use your webcam to take a photo or upload an image to the viewfinder below. Then, hit convert and see how the model tries to make it look like van Gogh painted your image!
